Integrating Perspectives on Discourse Annotation

Reflecting the growing interest in analyzing language beyond the sentence level in theoretical and computational linguistics, psycholinguistics, and applied domains, this workshop aims at bringing together researchers from the various subdisciplines that are working on aspects of discourse annotation. Advances in formal pragmatics are extending the empirical reach of linguistic analyses, and in computational linguistics interest in applications such as dialogue systems and argumentation mining is connecting to early research on dialogue structure and Rhetorical Structure Theory, and multi-layer corpus annotation efforts such as NXT Switchboard or the Penn Discourse Treebank. On the content side this includes, for example, research on i) the annotation of discourse relations (including argumentation mining), ii) the annotation of information structural concepts (topic, focus, ...) , and iii) the analysis and annotation of Questions under Discussion. We also aim to bring together different annotation methods, such as i) manual annotation (e.g., work focusing on the evaluation of annotations schemes and the reliability of the annotation in from of agreement values), ii) crowdsourcing annotation, potentially supporting the annotation of some aspects for larger data sets, and iii) the automatic labelling of discourse information. The workshop should thus provide an overview on the current state-of-the-art in the field of discourse annotation and foster the interaction and cooperation of researchers working in different frameworks and using different annotations methods.

The past years have seen a growing interest in analyzing language beyond the sentence level, in theoretical linguistics, computational linguistics, as well as in psycho-linguistics. The developments are driven by a range of factors, from increased interest in authentic language use in context to theoretical and practical motivations for the integration of top-down and bottom-up analysis.
